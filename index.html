Program Overview [cite: 1]

Starting point: 2–3 strict pull-ups [cite: 1] Goal: First strict muscle-up [cite: 1] Split: Strength Mon/Wed/Fri (48h recovery) · Cardio Tue/Thu/Sat/Sun [cite: 1]

Equipment needed: Pull-up bar, dip bars or rings, dumbbells, barbell, resistance bands (buy a set of 2–3 loop bands — they're the single most useful tool for this goal) [cite: 1].

Cardio Days (Fat Loss) [cite: 1]

  • Tuesday: Incline treadmill walk, 30–40 min, 10–12% incline, brisk pace (Zone 2 HR) [cite: 1]
  • Thursday: Jump rope intervals: 30 sec on / 30 sec off x 15–20 rounds [cite: 1]
  • Saturday: Longer incline walk (45 min) OR light jump rope + false-grip hang practice — active recovery, low intensity [cite: 1]
  • Sunday: Full rest, or a gentle walk. Prioritize sleep here. [cite: 1]

Notes That Matter [cite: 1]

  • Recovery: 48h between strength sessions is correct for your level — don't add a 4th strength day yet. Muscle-up training is joint-intensive (wrists, elbows, shoulders); more isn't better right now. [cite: 1]
  • Bands: Get 2–3 resistance band loops (light/medium/heavy). They're used for assisted pull-ups, assisted dips, and eventually assisted muscle-up transitions. [cite: 1]
  • Rings vs. bar: If you can access gymnastic rings, the muscle-up turnover is often easier to learn there than on a fixed bar (rings rotate with your wrists). Not mandatory — bar works fine too. [cite: 1]
  • Protein: Since you're combining strength + daily cardio, aim for roughly 1.6–2g protein per kg bodyweight to support recovery. [cite: 1]
  • Sleep: 7–8 hours — this is where the actual strength adaptation happens, especially for connective tissue around the shoulders/wrists. [cite: 1]
  • Deload: Every 5th–6th week, cut volume by ~40% for that week (fewer sets, same movements) to let joints catch up. [cite: 1]
  • Track weekly: Max pull-ups, max dips, and false-grip hang time. These three numbers tell you exactly when to move phases. [cite: 1]
