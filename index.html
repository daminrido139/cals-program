20-Week Calisthenics + Strength Recomposition Program[cite: 1]

Profile: 21M · 167 cm · 69 kg · ~26% BF · 2–3 strict pull-ups[cite: 1]

Goals: 26% → 12% body fat · aesthetic, balanced physique · first strict muscle-up[cite: 1]

Training days: Mon, Tue, Wed, Fri, Sat (5x/week) · Rest: Thu, Sun[cite: 1]

Cardio: AM (fasted or light-fed) · Lifting/calisthenics: PM[cite: 1]

1. The Math Behind the Goal[cite: 1]

  • Current: 69 kg @ 26% BF → ~18 kg fat / ~51 kg lean[cite: 1]
  • Target: 12% BF @ same (or slightly higher) lean mass → ~58 kg total[cite: 1]
  • Net loss needed: ~11 kg over 20 weeks (~0.5 kg/week average)[cite: 1]

This is realistic if nutrition is consistent — but it won't be linear.[cite: 1] Weeks 1–10 will move faster (more fat to lose), weeks 15–20 will feel slower (this is normal, not a plateau to panic over).[cite: 1] If you add muscle simultaneously (very likely, since you're relatively new to structured training), the scale will lag behind how you actually look — trust tape measurements and photos as much as the scale.[cite: 1]

Reassess every 5 weeks: weight, waist measurement, and ideally another InBody/caliper reading.[cite: 1] Adjust calories ±150–200 kcal based on actual rate of change, not the plan on paper.[cite: 1]

4. The 20 Weeks in 4 Blocks[cite: 1]

Each block = 4 working weeks + 1 lighter "deload/diet-break" week (drop volume ~40%, eat at maintenance calories).[cite: 1] This protects joints, resets hormones, and gives you a mental breather.[cite: 1]

Block[cite: 1] Weeks[cite: 1] Focus[cite: 1]
1 — Foundation[cite: 1] 1–5[cite: 1] Build work capacity, bulletproof form, grow pull-up max (target: 6–8 reps by week 5)[cite: 1]
2 — Strength & Skill[cite: 1] 6–10[cite: 1] Heavier barbell loads, introduce false-grip work and banded muscle-up transitions[cite: 1]
3 — Intensification[cite: 1] 11–15[cite: 1] Higher pull volume, low-ring/jumping muscle-up transitions, visible leaning-out[cite: 1]
4 — Peak[cite: 1] 16–20[cite: 1] Strict muscle-up attempts, tempo/definition work, final push to 12%[cite: 1]

Week 5, 10, 15, 20 = deload/diet-break weeks.[cite: 1]

5. Muscle-Up Roadmap (from 2–3 pull-ups)[cite: 1]

Phase[cite: 1] What to add on Monday's "muscle-up progression" slot[cite: 1]
Weeks 1–5[cite: 1] Explosive pull-ups (chest to bar), false-grip dead hangs 4x15–20s[cite: 1]
Weeks 6–10[cite: 1] False-grip pull-ups, banded low transitions on rings[cite: 1]
Weeks 11–15[cite: 1] Jumping muscle-ups (rings/bar), straight bar kip-free transition drills[cite: 1]
Weeks 16–20[cite: 1] Banded strict muscle-up attempts → unassisted attempts[cite: 1]

Realistic target: your first strict muscle-up somewhere in weeks 14–20, depending on relative strength gains.[cite: 1] Chest-to-bar pull-up strength (aim: 8–10 clean reps) is the single best predictor — prioritize that over transition drilling early on.[cite: 1]

6. Nutrition Guardrails[cite: 1]

Estimated maintenance (Mifflin-St Jeor, moderate activity): ~2,500 kcal/day[cite: 1]

Target[cite: 1] Amount[cite: 1]
Daily calories (cut)[cite: 1] ~2,000–2,100 kcal (400–500 kcal deficit)[cite: 1]
Protein[cite: 1] 150–160g/day (~2.2g/kg) — non-negotiable for muscle retention[cite: 1]
Carbs[cite: 1] 200–250g/day, concentrated around training[cite: 1]
Fats[cite: 1] 55–65g/day[cite: 1]
  • Recheck bodyweight trend weekly (average, not single day-to-day readings).[cite: 1]
  • If weight loss stalls for 2+ weeks with adherence confirmed, drop ~150 kcal.[cite: 1]
  • On deload weeks, eat at maintenance (~2,500 kcal) — this isn't optional, it's what makes the next block productive.[cite: 1]

7. Non-Negotiables[cite: 1]

  • Sleep 7–8 hrs — this affects fat loss and recovery more than any single workout.[cite: 1]
  • Protein at every meal — hardest thing to fix retroactively.[cite: 1]
  • Don't skip face pulls / scapular work — pull-up and dip-heavy programs wreck shoulders without them.[cite: 1]
  • Track pull-up max weekly — it's your best real-time progress signal alongside the scale/tape.[cite: 1]
