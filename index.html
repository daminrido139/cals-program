2. Is 26% → 15% BF in 20 weeks realistic?

Yes — and it's actually a conservative target, not an aggressive one [cite: 1]. Here's the math [cite: 1]:

  • If your lean mass stays roughly where it is (or grows slightly from training), hitting 15% BF means a bodyweight of ~59–61 kg [cite: 1].
  • That means losing ~8.5–9 kg of fat mass over 20 weeks = ~0.45 kg/week (~1 lb/week) [cite: 1].
  • 0.45 kg/week is a gentle, muscle-sparing rate of fat loss — well within what's achievable with a moderate deficit + resistance training + high protein, and it leaves plenty of room for beginner muscle gain ("newbie gains") to happen at the same time [cite: 1]. This is a genuine recomposition, not just a cut [cite: 1].

The muscle-up is the more skill-dependent goal, but 20 weeks is a realistic runway from 2–3 strict pull-ups if you're consistent — see the phased plan below [cite: 1].

4. Cardio

Goal: burn an extra ~300–400 kcal per session, 4–5x/week, without eating into recovery for calisthenics [cite: 1].

  • Incline treadmill walk: 12–15% incline, 5–5.5 km/h, 25–35 min [cite: 1]
  • Jump rope: 3–4 rounds of 3 min work / 1 min rest (12–15 min total), lower impact fatigue but very time-efficient [cite: 1]
  • Place cardio after strength training or on separate light days — never before pulling/push work, since fatigued grip and shoulders sabotage pull-up quality [cite: 1].

7. Phase 2 — Strength & Volume (Weeks 7–13)

Goal: 10–12 strict pull-ups, 10+ dips, first false-grip work [cite: 1]

Key changes from Phase 1 [cite: 1]:

  • Drop the negatives — you shouldn't need them anymore [cite: 1].
  • Add chest-to-bar pull-ups and slow-tempo (3 sec down) reps for strength [cite: 1].
  • Start false-grip dead hangs: 3 x 10–15 sec, building toward 20 sec [cite: 1].
  • Add light weighted or backpack-loaded dips/pull-ups once bodyweight reps exceed 10 [cite: 1].

8. Phase 3 — Muscle-Up Specialization (Weeks 14–20)

Goal: clean, controlled muscle-up [cite: 1]

Key changes from Phase 2 [cite: 1]:

  • Monday and Thursday pull days now center on transition work: negative muscle-ups from top support (3–5 sec lower), jumping muscle-ups on a low bar, band-assisted full reps [cite: 1].
  • Keep 1–2 sets of straight strict pull-ups and dips per week just to maintain base strength — don't abandon them [cite: 1].
  • By week 17–18, attempt unassisted muscle-up singles at the start of the pull session when fresh (this is a skill best trained un-fatigued) [cite: 1].
  • Weeks 19–20: taper training volume slightly, prioritize quality reps and rest, keep attempting clean muscle-ups 2x/week [cite: 1].

10. Notes

  • Sunday is rest — actual rest, not another cardio session [cite: 1]. Recovery is where the muscle-up strength is built [cite: 1].
